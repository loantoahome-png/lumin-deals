# Lumin — Calling & Texting Compliance Quick Reference

**For:** Efrain, Matt, Moe · **Last updated:** 2026-06-18

> ⚠️ **Not legal advice.** Plain-English cheat sheet for day-to-day outreach. TCPA/DNC
> violations run **$500–$1,500 per call or text**, so confirm your actual policy with
> compliance counsel before relying on this. State rules vary by where the borrower lives.

---

## The #1 thing to understand: calls and texts are TWO different rule sets

| | **Phone CALLS** | **Automated TEXTS / robocalls** |
|---|---|---|
| Governed by | DNC Registry + EBR exemptions | **TCPA prior express written consent (PEWC)** |
| The "3-month" clock | ✅ applies (see below) | ❌ **does NOT apply** — wrong yardstick |
| What lets you contact | An inquiry/relationship exemption *or* not-on-DNC | A valid written consent that lasts **until revoked** |

**Do not use the 3-month window to decide whether you can text. They're unrelated.**

---

## CALLS — the DNC windows

A number on the National DNC Registry is **still callable** if an exemption applies:

- **Inquiry exemption — 3 months.** Borrower submitted a mortgage inquiry/lead form →
  you may call for **3 months** even if they're on the DNC list.
- **Established Business Relationship — 18 months.** Borrower actually transacted with you →
  **18 months** from the last transaction.
- **After the window:** the exemption lapses. If they're on the DNC list, **stop calling**
  unless you have separate consent.

**Always overrides any exemption:**
- **Company-specific DNC** — if they told *us* to stop, that's **permanent**.
- **State DNC lists** — some of our states are stricter than federal.

*We do not currently run a DNC scrubber (deferred — cost). So: stay inside the inquiry
window for cold calls, honor every opt-out, and treat aged leads (past 3 months) as
higher-risk until we can scrub.*

---

## TEXTS (and autodialed/robocalls) — TCPA consent

- Automated marketing texts to a cell need **valid prior express written consent (PEWC)**.
- **PEWC does not expire at 3 months.** It lasts **until the borrower revokes it.**
  → So: *valid consent + they haven't opted out = you can keep texting*, past 3 months.

**The catch — an inquiry is NOT automatically consent to text.**
PEWC requires the lead form to have had clear, conspicuous written language agreeing to
**automated marketing texts**, naming who's contacting them, by a checkbox/signature.

✅ **Action:** confirm our vendors (FRU, Lendgo, LMB, Lending Tree, LeadPoint, OwnUp) capture
PEWC and can produce a **TrustedForm or Jornaya certificate** for each lead. No cert = no
defensible texting consent.

---

## Always applies (consent or not)

- **Honor opt-outs made *any* reasonable way** — not just "STOP." Includes "please stop" said
  on a call. Must honor within ~10 business days.
- **A2P 10DLC** registration for texting campaigns (carrier requirement, separate from TCPA).
- **Quiet hours:** no contact before **8am / after 9pm** the borrower's local time.
- **Identify yourself** (name + company) on calls and texts.
- **State mini-TCPAs** (Florida FTSA, Washington, Oklahoma, etc.) — some stricter than federal.

## Quick decision cheat

| Situation | OK? |
|---|---|
| Manual call, fresh lead, inquired < 3 mo ago | ✅ Lowest risk |
| Manual call, lead inquired > 3 mo ago, on DNC | ⛔ Not without separate consent / scrub |
| Automated text, valid PEWC cert, not opted out | ✅ (regardless of the 3-month clock) |
| Automated text, only an "inquiry" but no PEWC cert | ⛔ Get the cert first |
| Anyone who told us to stop (any channel, any way) | ⛔ Permanent — never contact |

- **GHL DND flags** surface opt-outs in the dashboard (the red DND badge).
- **Inquiry window** covers fresh-lead cold calls.
- **Vendor consent certs** (verify we're getting them) cover texting.
